Children with asthma or allergies are also more at risk of developing sleep apnea.
Children who do not sleep well may have a sleep problem.
Children who have sleep apnea almost always snore or have heavy, labored breathing.
Children can be misdiagnosed with a learning disorder or with ADHD when the problem is really an underlying sleep disorder.
If children have sleep apnea, they often have difficulty concentrating, paying attention and remembering what has been learned.
